20 Question 20

Language: Intermediate Student with Lambda

Preparation: Module 7

In this question, you will implement computations using the binary definition of Ints ("two’s complement notation") from lecture, but translated into Racket lists in a manner similar to Question 19.

We will represent Z by '(z), N by '(n), A x by (cons 'o y), and B x by (cons 'i y), where y is the representation of x. This is a recursive definition of slist suitable for representing all integers, and leads to a notion of structural recursion on slists which you should use for your solutions.

Write the functions int-to-slist and slist-to-int, which are the translations of fromInt and toInt, which in turn are the generalizations of fromNat and toNat from lecture. Here "int" refers to the usual representation of integers in Racket. As an example, (slist-to-int '(i i o n)) should evaluate to -5. These functions are useful for testing. You should not use them (or built-in integers in any way) in the body of the three functions described below.

Next, write add, which consumes two slists and produces an slist, and is the translation of the version of plus in lecture module 07 that works on the binary definition of Ints.

Finally, write neg and minus (subtraction). As in Question 19, your functions must use structural recursion, and must be efficient.

To submit: Q20.rkt.
